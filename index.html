About Me
Bio

Moses is an International Trade and Development Professional with over 5 years’ cumulative experience supporting management and implementation of trade-related research and economic development projects in Africa, Caribbean and Pacific (ACP).

His experience ranges from research on multilateral trade negotiations, governance, international affairs, trade and sustainable development, regional integration and trade policy analysis. He has worked on trade-related technical assistance (TA) projects commissioned by organisations including the European Union / Commission, the Commonwealth Secretariat, UNDP, the African Union, DFID, and various governments and think tanks.

About Me
Experience

His areas of expertise include advanced research on international political economy, trade and development, governance, regional integration including the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA), policy and regulatory frameworks and Africa’s international relations and EU – Africa / ACP trade.

Furthermore, he is proficient in project / programme management (design, grant writing, bidding processes and proposal development, implementation, M&E and theory of change, project life cycles and logical frameworks). Furthermore, he is specialised in Human-Centred Designing Thinking (HCD), and its application to policy making and programming.

Born and raised in Kenya, Moses has an extensive international experience and network having studied and lived in four countries (Kenya, Mauritius, South Africa and the United States) and travelled to over 20 countries in East and Southern Africa, the Caribbean and Latin America. In addition to gaining wide perspectives, these improved his agility, intercultural awareness and empathy, provided an opportunity to understand the world, meet with people whose lives we are trying to improve and expand his networks.

In Addition
More About Me

In addition to working on international trade projects, he spends his time working with social innovators designing leadership programmes for youths and youth empowerment, travel and internationalisation strategies. For example, he is presently working on establishing a leadership-based Scholarship and Entrepreneurship Fund targeting former street-involved youths (street children).

Moses is available for full-time or part-time roles and projects (including consultancies), in thematic areas above. He is also available for speaking (panels and seminars) and training engagements in international trade and development, governance and public policy, internationalisation and education, entrepreneurship and social change, mentoring and coaching youths and other related collaborations.

Education

Moses obtained a MA in International Relations from the University of Cape Town, a Bachelor of science (with honours) Political Science from the University of Mauritius and was a Graduate Exchange Student at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst where he took courses in Economics and Global Health.

Awards

Moses is a Dalai Lama Fellow (2019), a Mandela Rhodes Scholar (2017), and a Mauritius Africa Scholar (2013).
